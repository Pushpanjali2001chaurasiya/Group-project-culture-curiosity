History of Varanasi

History, Best Places to Visit and Travel Guide of Varanasi – One of Seven Sacred Cities of Hinduism, Uttar Pradesh, India

Varanasi also known as Banaras or Kashi, one of the Sapta puri of Hinduism and abode of Lord Shiva is located on bank of sacred river Ganga in state of Uttar Pradesh. Varanasi is believed to be oldest living city of earth and home to Kashi Vishwanath Jyotirlinga temple. From the ancient time, Varanasi holds great religious importance in Hinduism and one of the major pilgrimage center. As Varanasi is continuously populous city from thousands of year, thus it became the cultural capital of India. Pilgrims and tourist take the holy bath in sacred river Ganga here in Kashi and it is said to give the salvation and freedom from the cycle of birth and re birth. Varanasi is also key pilgrimage from Jain community as well as is believed to be birthplace of Parsvanath, the twenty-third Tirthankar. Kashi is also important for the Buddhism as lord Buddha first taught Dharama at Sarnath, just 10 kilometres north-east of Varanasi near the confluence of the Ganges and the Varuna rivers in Uttar Pradesh, India. Varanasi played vital role in education, spiritualism, Sanskrit, yoga and Hindi language from centuries. Tulsi Das had written the Ram Charit Manas – life of Lord Shree Ram here in Varanasi.

Along with Kashi Vishwnath temple, you can find small and big temple at every corner of Varanasi also there are more than 80 Ghats built on bank of river Ganga which used for Puja – rituals and holy bath. Dashashwamedh Ghat, Manikarnika Ghat, Assi Ghat, Tulsi Ghat, PanchaGanga Ghat, Jain Ghat or Bachraj Ghat, Hanuman Ghat, Raj Ghat, Lalita Ghat, Scindia Ghat etc. are important Ghat of Varanasi. Kaal Bhairav temple, Durga Temple also popular as Monkey temple, Gauri Mata temple, Nepali Hindu Temple, Sarnath, Tulsi Manas Mandir, Ratneshwar Temple, Annapurna Temple, Bharat Mata temple etc. are other important religious place in Varanasi.

Millions of Tourist across the world visit Varanasi throughout the year with great devotion towards the Lord Shiva and also to know and learn about India Culture and Spirituality. Mahashivratri, Sravan month, Ganga Festival, Chhat Puja, Diwali etc. festival are celebrated here with amazing enthusiasm. Due to heavy rush of devotee across the year, you can find lot of hotels, Dharmashala, Guest house in Varanasi to accommodate tourist. Also you can find lot of restaurant in Varanasi. Banarasi Saree, Banarasi Paan, Banarasi snacks, dress, jewelry, street food etc. are another attraction here which you must not forgot during Varanasi trip.
