History, Best Places to Visit and Travel Guide of Varanasi – One of Seven Sacred Cities of Hinduism, Uttar Pradesh, India

Varanasi also known as Banaras or Kashi, one of the Sapta puri of Hinduism and abode of Lord Shiva is located on bank of sacred river Ganga in state of Uttar Pradesh. Varanasi is believed to be oldest living city of earth and home to Kashi Vishwanath Jyotirlinga temple.
The city is host to numerous religious festivals. Mahashivaratri, the great night of the god Shiva, is celebrated by a procession from the Mahamrityunjaya Temple to the Kashi Vishwanath Temple. The Ganga festival in November or December is dedicated to the goddess of the Ganges River, considered sacred by all Hindus. Thousands of lamps are placed on the ghats and set afloat on the river. The festival of Bharat Milap in October or November commemorates the reunion of Lord Rama with his younger brother Bharat after 14 years of exile. A five-day festival of dhrupad (classical Indian vocal style) in March attracts renowned artists from all over India to the city’s Tulsi Ghat along the river.
