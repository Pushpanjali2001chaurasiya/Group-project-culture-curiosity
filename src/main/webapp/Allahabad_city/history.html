History of Prayagraj

History, Culture, Best Places to Visit and Travel Guide of Prayagraj, Major Pilgrimage Destination of Uttar Pradesh, India

Prayagraj, formerly known as Allahabad or Ilahabad is believed to be one of the oldest city of India and very important religious destination for Hinduism. As per the ancient sacred text of Sanatan Dharma, Prayagraj is mentioned as Prayag or Teerthraj which literally mean is king of all the teertha or pilgrimage center. Prayagraj is located on the confluence of holy river Ganga, Yamuna and Saraswati. Prayagraj is the place where Gange, Yamune and Gupt Saraswati meets which is also known as Triveni Sangam, the highly sacred place of Hinduism. This is the place where great Kumbh Mela held in every six years and Mahakumbh in every 12 years which largest assemblies of pilgrims across the world. Millions of devotee across the world flocked here at Prayagraj during the gran Kumbh Mela and takes the holy bath here at Triveni Sangam. It is also believed that Lord Brahma had performed the very first yagya here. Thus Prayagraj hold same importance as Varanasi and Haridwar

Along with the religious importance of Prayagraj, it is headquarter of Prayagraj district, Uttar Pradesh, India and one of important and populous city of India. The ancient city of Prayagraj was known as Kosambi in the late Vedic period, named by the Kuru rulers of Hastinapur and developed as their capital. The same importance continues during the Maurya Empire and Gupta Empire as well. The importance of this region is mentioned in the sacred text of Buddhism as well.

There are lots of places to visit here in Prayagraj, in which Triveni Sangam is the most popular one. Apart from it you must plan to visit Museum, Fort, Anand Bhavan, Hanuman Mandir, Patalpuri Temple, Kaushambi etc. The best time to visit here is during the September to March and during the Magh Mela, Maha Kumbh Mela and Dussehra.

How to Reach Prayagraj, Triveni Sangam of Ganga, Yamua and Saraswati, King of Pilgrimage, Uttar Pradesh, India

Prayagraj is one of the important holy site of Hinduism, the place of Triveni Sangam of Ganga, Yamuna, Saraswati and famous district of state of Uttar Pradesh, India. Thus you can easily reach here via Road, Rail and Air network from across the India. Thousands of devotee and tourist across the world flocked here around the year and millions of devotee across the world during the Kumbh Mela. Prayagraj is very well connected with all major cities of India via Road, Rail and Air network. The Triveni Sangam is located at just 7 km away from the Prayagraj railway station and you can reach here from any corner of cities via cab or rickshaw. Prayagraj has major railway junction from where you can get train to all the cities of India like Delhi, Mumbai, Ahmedabad, Chennai, Indore, Allahabad, Dehradun etc. at very high frequency. Prayagraj has airport from where you can get directly flight from all major cities of India. Also Prayagraj is important district of Uttar Pradesh thus it is well connected by state and national highway. Prayagraj is located at 167 km from Ayodhya, 300 km from Gorakhpur, 200 km from Lucknow, 120 km from Varanasi, 204 km from Kanpur and 120 km from Chitrakoot
