History, Culture, Best Places to Visit and Travel Guide of Ayodhya, Major Pilgrimage Destination of Uttar Pradesh, India

Ayodhya, the birthplace of Lord Shri Ram and one of the Saptpuri is major pilgrimage center of Uttar Pradesh, India. Ayodhya, also known as Awadh or Awadhpuri or Oudh or Saket, the ancient historical city is headquarter of Ayodhya District and located on bank of holy river Saryu. It hold high religious importance in Hinduism as it is the birth place of Lord Shri Ram, the seventh incarnation of Lord Vishnu and also closely associated with Ramayana the great Indian epic. Thus Ayodhya is famed as one of the seven sacred cities of Hinduism. Ayodhya was the capital of the descendants of the Surya dynasty, the legendary Ikshvaku of the ancient Kosala Kingdom an
Ayodhya is considered one of the oldest cities in India. It served as the capital of the ancient Kosala Kingdom.
The city is famously associated with the epic Ramayana, where it is described as the birthplace of Lord Rama, one of the most revered deities in Hinduism.
Ayodhya is the center of grand celebrations during festivals like Diwali, Dussehra, and Ram Navami, attracting pilgrims and tourists from across the world.
